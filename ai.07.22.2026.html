Model releases & capability updates

Google
Google ships Gemini 3.6 Flash, 3.5 Flash-Lite and 3.5 Flash Cyber, teases Gemini 4 pretraining [source]
On July 21, Google released three new Gemini models — 3.6 Flash, 3.5 Flash-Lite, and a security-tuned 3.5 Flash Cyber restricted to governments and trusted partners — while confirming Gemini 3.5 Pro is still testing with partners and disclosing it has begun its largest-ever pretraining run for Gemini 4.
Why it matters: Microsoft partners building on Azure AI Foundry or Copilot Studio with Gemini connectors should note the delayed 3.5 Pro timeline and evaluate the new Flash-tier models for cost-sensitive Power Platform automation scenarios.
Alibaba / Qwen
Alibaba previews Qwen3.8-Max, a 2.4-trillion-parameter multimodal model [source]
At the World AI Conference in Shanghai on July 19, Alibaba's Qwen team previewed Qwen3.8-Max, a 2.4T-parameter sparse Mixture-of-Experts model with a 1M-token context window, its first multimodal model above 1 trillion parameters. No benchmark card or license accompanied the preview.
Why it matters: A reminder of how fast the open-weight/low-cost tier is moving; relevant for partners evaluating multi-model strategies in Foundry model routing, though enterprise governance backing is still unproven.

Pricing / API / availability changes

DeepSeek
DeepSeek V4 reaches general availability with peak/off-peak pricing [source]
DeepSeek V4 moved from preview to GA on July 19 in Pro and Flash variants with a 1M-token context window. The launch introduces peak-hour pricing (2x baseline during Beijing business hours) with V4-Pro at $0.435/M input and $0.87/M output tokens; legacy chat/reasoner endpoints retire July 24.
Why it matters: Continued downward price pressure from Chinese frontier labs is worth tracking for any Power Platform / Copilot Studio cost-optimization conversations that weigh model choice against Azure-hosted options.

Product & platform launches

AWS
OpenAI's GPT-5.6 (Sol, Terra, Luna) models land on Amazon Bedrock [source]
AWS's July 20 weekly roundup confirmed OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna models are now available on Amazon Bedrock, continuing AWS's push to broaden its Bedrock model catalog beyond its own and Anthropic's models.
