AI Vendor Digest

Jun 22 – Jun 24, 2026

Pricing / API / availability changes

Anthropic
Claude Fable 5 exits complimentary window, moves to usage-credit pricing [source]
Starting June 23, Claude Fable 5 is no longer included in Pro, Max, Team, or seat-based Enterprise subscriptions. Access now requires usage credits, priced at $10 per million input tokens and $50 per million output tokens — double the cost of Claude Opus 4.8 ($5/$25). The complimentary window had been offered since Fable 5's June 9 launch.
Why it matters: Microsoft 365 Copilot and Azure AI Foundry both offer Anthropic models (including Fable 5) as selectable backends. The pricing shift means D365 / Power Platform workloads routing through Anthropic endpoints will see higher per-token costs if Fable 5 is selected; partners should verify model selection settings in Copilot Studio connectors.

Product & platform launches

OpenAI
GPT-5.5-Cyber goes fully live; Patch the Planet open-source vulnerability initiative launched [source]
On June 22, OpenAI expanded its Daybreak cybersecurity platform with three releases: the full (non-preview) availability of GPT-5.5-Cyber, which scores 85.6% on the CyberGym vulnerability-reproduction benchmark versus 81.8% for standard GPT-5.5; a Codex Security plugin for developer workflows; and Patch the Planet, an open-source patching program built with Trail of Bits and HackerOne targeting widely used projects including cURL, Go, and Python. A vendor partner program was also opened to CrowdStrike, Sophos, Fortinet, Tenable, IBM, and Check Point.
Why it matters: Power Platform and Azure-hosted solutions that use Codex or OpenAI APIs for code generation may benefit from Codex Security's integrated vulnerability scanning. The partner program also signals deeper security-vendor integrations that could surface in Microsoft Security Copilot over time.
OpenAI
ChatGPT Enterprise / Edu gains Slack connector actions [source]
ChatGPT Enterprise and Edu workspaces can now enable action-taking inside Slack: joining channels, creating reminders, uploading files, and updating Slack profiles directly from a ChatGPT conversation. Admins control which actions are available via Action control in the Apps settings panel.
Why it matters: For Microsoft 365 shops, this is a notable Copilot competitor move — OpenAI is building the kind of cross-app agentic workflows Microsoft is rolling out via Copilot Actions. Partners advising on productivity tooling should be aware customers may compare ChatGPT Enterprise's Slack integration against Copilot's Teams-native capabilities.

Partnerships, funding, acquisitions

SpaceX / Reflection AI
SpaceX signs $6.3B compute deal with open-source AI startup Reflection AI [source]
SpaceX inked a multi-year agreement on June 22 to provide Reflection AI with access to Nvidia GB300 chips at its Colossus 2 data center, with monthly payments of approximately $150 million beginning July 1, 2026 and a total contract value of up to $6.3 billion through 2029. Reflection AI is developing open-source frontier models, positioning the deal as a bid to compete with proprietary labs at scale.
Why it matters: While not directly tied to D365 or Power Platform, the deal illustrates the accelerating capital flowing into open-source frontier AI. Open-source frontier models lower the barrier for custom model deployment on Azure, which affects how partners design Azure AI Foundry solutions for Dynamics workloads.
