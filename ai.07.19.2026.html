Model releases & capability updates

NVIDIA
Cosmos 3 Edge brings on-device robot intelligence; Japan's manufacturing leaders join the Cosmos Coalition [source]
NVIDIA unveiled Cosmos 3 Edge, a 4-billion-parameter world model built on Nemotron that runs on edge GPUs and Jetson hardware for real-time on-device vision reasoning and robot policy generation. Alongside it, Japanese manufacturing and robotics leaders — including Fujitsu, Kawasaki Heavy Industries, FANUC, Hitachi, and Yaskawa — committed to join the NVIDIA Cosmos Coalition to build open, frontier physical-AI models.
Why it matters: Edge-capable world models point toward AI moving off the cloud and onto shop-floor and field hardware — relevant to D365 Field Service and IoT scenarios where latency or connectivity rule out cloud-only Copilot experiences.

Pricing / API / availability changes

Anthropic
Mid-conversation system messages ship on the Claude API, Bedrock, and Google Cloud [source]
Anthropic now lets developers insert a role:"system" message partway through a conversation, instead of only at the top of the prompt, so agents can add new operator-level instructions mid-session without invalidating the cached prefix. It's live on Claude Fable 5, Claude Mythos 5, and Claude Opus 4.8 across the Claude API, Amazon Bedrock, and Google Cloud, with no beta header required.
Why it matters: Power Platform and Copilot Studio builders running long agentic Claude sessions via API or Bedrock can inject policy or context changes mid-run without paying a full cache-miss penalty — a real cost lever for high-volume agent flows.

Microsoft and 3M strike strategic partnership on AI data-center infrastructure [source]
Microsoft and 3M announced a strategic partnership on July 15 under which Azure becomes the first announced hyperscaler to deploy 3M's Expanded Beam Optical (EBO) fiber-connector technology in its data centers, while 3M in turn adopts Microsoft's AI and digital platforms across its own enterprise operations. The deal pairs Microsoft's hyperscale infrastructure with 3M's materials science and manufacturing to support denser AI and cloud workloads.
Why it matters: Data-center capacity and reliability investments like this directly affect the availability and performance ceiling of Azure-hosted Copilot, Microsoft Foundry, and Dynamics 365 AI workloads that partners build on.

Notable research or safety news

OpenAI
GPT-Red: automated red-teamer outperforms humans 84% to 13% on prompt injection [source]
OpenAI detailed GPT-Red, an in-house automated red-teaming model that pits attacking and defending AIs against each other to find and fix vulnerabilities before deployment. In tests on novel safety scenarios, GPT-Red found exploits at an 84% success rate versus 13% for human red-teamers, and models adversarially trained with it — including GPT-5.6 — showed six times fewer failures on prompt-injection benchmarks with no drop in general capability.
Why it matters: Prompt injection is a top risk for agentic Copilot and Power Platform deployments that call external tools and data; more robust base models reduce, but don't eliminate, the need for your own guardrails.
