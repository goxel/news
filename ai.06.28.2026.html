GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna unveiled in government-approved limited preview [source]
On June 26, OpenAI launched three models under the GPT-5.6 family: Sol (flagship, $5 input / $30 output per 1M tokens), Terra (balanced, $2.50 / $15), and Luna (fast/affordable, $1 / $6). Product & platform launches

OpenAI
Jalapeño: OpenAI and Broadcom unveil OpenAI's first custom LLM inference chip [source]
On June 24, OpenAI and Broadcom jointly announced Jalapeño, a custom ASIC designed specifically for LLM inference. Co-developed in just nine months, the chip targets substantially better performance-per-watt than current GPU solutions and is planned for initial deployment by end of 2026. Early benchmarks suggest ~50% lower inference cost vs. equivalent NVIDIA GPU workloads.
Why it matters: As OpenAI's primary infrastructure partner, Microsoft (Azure OpenAI Service) will benefit from Jalapeño-level efficiency at scale. Lower inference costs eventually translate into lower per-call pricing for Copilot and Power Platform AI Builder workloads.

Anthropic
Anthropic accuses Alibaba of running a 28.8 million-exchange distillation campaign against Claude [source]
On June 24, Anthropic sent a letter to US senators and White House officials accusing Alibaba's Qwen AI lab of the largest known model-distillation attack against a US AI provider: ~25,000 fraudulent accounts conducted 28.8 million exchanges with Claude between April 22 and June 5, 2026, targeting software-engineering and agentic-reasoning capabilities. Anthropic called it a 'brazenly illicit' effort to extract US frontier-model capabilities for a Chinese competitor.
Why it matters: For enterprise customers using Anthropic models via Azure AI Foundry or Copilot, this underscores the importance of audit logging, access controls, and monitoring for anomalous query patterns. It is also likely to accelerate US regulatory pressure on AI export controls and enterprise security requirements.
