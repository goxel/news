Anthropic
Anthropic finds a small internal 'workspace' that Claude uses before answering [source]
A new interpretability paper, 'Verbalizable Representations Form a Global Workspace in Language Models,' describes a low-dimensional internal space ('J-space') found via a new technique (the Jacobian lens) that appears to broker between Claude's silent computation and what it verbalizes. The workspace accounts for roughly 10% of activation variance and includes signs the model can represent that it is being evaluated.
Why it matters: Interpretability progress like this feeds directly into AI governance and audit conversations that matter for regulated D365 customers (financial services, government, healthcare) assessing AI safety and testing practices for Copilot-based agents.
