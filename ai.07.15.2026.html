Jul 12 – Jul 15, 2026

Model releases & capability updates

Google
Google unveils Gemma 4 E2B on-device model and Tensor ML SDK beta for Pixel 10 [source]
At Google I/O Connect India, Google announced Gemma 4 E2B, a lightweight model that runs natively and fully offline on the Pixel 10's custom Tensor SoC, enabling on-device multimodal chat, real-time image recognition, and agentic "Mobile Actions" (e.g. controlling Wi-Fi or maps by voice) with data never leaving the device. Google also opened beta access to a Tensor ML SDK so developers can build similar on-device experiences.
Why it matters: Reflects the industry's continued shift toward small, private, on-device models as an alternative to cloud-hosted assistants — useful context for partners assessing where Microsoft's Copilot+ PC / on-device AI strategy sits relative to competitors.

Product & platform launches

Anthropic
Anthropic launches Claude for Teachers, giving US K-12 educators free access to Claude [source]
Anthropic introduced Claude for Teachers, giving verified U.S. K-12 educators free access to premium Claude tools, teaching Skills, Cowork, and curriculum connectors including Learning Commons, mapped to all 50 states' academic standards. Teacher and student data is excluded from model training and is covered by a K-12 Data Processing Addendum written to comply with FERPA; Anthropic is piloting an evaluation of the program with Detroit Public Schools.
Why it matters: Shows Anthropic packaging strict data-governance guarantees (no training on customer data, compliance addenda) for a regulated vertical — a template pattern D365/Power Platform partners should watch as AI vendors court education, government, and healthcare customers.
Anthropic
Claude Artifacts adds public sharing, multiplayer editing, and Claude Tag creation from Slack [source]
Anthropic expanded Artifacts so Team and Enterprise users can publish via public shareable links (viewable by anyone, no Claude account required) and collaboratively edit the same artifact in real time instead of passing files back and forth. Claude Tag can now also spin up a persistent, shareable artifact directly from a Slack conversation.
Why it matters: Signals a move from single-player AI outputs toward persistent, governed team workspaces — directly comparable to how Power Platform/Copilot Studio position shared canvas apps and Copilot-built artifacts for teams.
Perplexity
Perplexity Computer adds persistent memory ("Brain"), faster Opus 4.8 mode, mid-task model switching, and website publishing [source]
Perplexity shipped a major Computer update: "Brain," a private context graph that persists across sessions, connectors and files (Perplexity reports 25% higher answer correctness and 13% lower cost on tasks with prior context); a new Fast mode for Opus 4.8; the ability to switch models mid-task; one-click website publishing to a pplx.app address or a connected Vercel domain; and private-company financial research sourced from Forge Global.
Why it matters: A competitive data point for partners evaluating AI agent platforms with persistent memory and built-in publishing/research tools alongside Copilot Studio and Power Platform agent offerings.
